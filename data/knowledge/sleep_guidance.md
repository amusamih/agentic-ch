# Sleep Guidance (Synthetic Knowledge Base)

## Recommended sleep duration (general)
- Most adults benefit from ~7–9 hours of sleep per night.
- Consistency (regular bed/wake times) is often more important than occasional long sleep.

## Sleep hygiene basics
- Keep a consistent sleep schedule (even on weekends).
- Limit caffeine later in the day; avoid heavy meals close to bedtime.
- Reduce screen exposure 60 minutes before bed.
- Ensure the sleep environment is dark, quiet, and cool.

## When sleep is short or disrupted
- Identify triggers: stress, travel, late work, late exercise, alcohol.
- Use a wind-down routine: light reading, relaxation breathing, meditation.
- If waking frequently: consider reducing late fluids and optimizing temperature/noise.

## Stress and sleep
- High stress can reduce sleep duration and sleep quality.
- Consider: journaling, mindfulness, exercise earlier in the day, and setting boundaries for late-night work.

## When to seek professional support
- If persistent insomnia, loud snoring, witnessed apnea, or daytime sleepiness impacts safety.
- If sleep issues are accompanied by concerning symptoms, seek clinician advice.
