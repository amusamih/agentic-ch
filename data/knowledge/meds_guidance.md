# Medication Safety Guidance (Curated KB for Prototype)

## How to interpret interaction severity
- **Major**: Potentially serious harm. Avoid combination or use only under clinician supervision.
- **Moderate**: Use caution; consider alternatives, timing adjustments, and monitoring.
- **Minor**: Limited clinical impact for most people; still monitor symptoms.

## What to do when an interaction is found
- **Do not change prescription doses** based on an assistant.
- If the interaction is **major**:
  - Prefer **avoidance** or clinician-directed alternatives.
  - Ask about **monitoring** (e.g., lab checks) and warning signs.
- If **moderate**:
  - Consider **safer OTC alternatives** if applicable.
  - Ask about **timing**, **duration**, and **monitoring**.

## Red flags (seek urgent help)
- Symptoms suggesting severe reaction or bleeding, chest pain, trouble breathing, fainting, severe swelling, confusion, uncontrolled vomiting.

## Example pattern: NSAID + anticoagulant
- Combining **NSAIDs (e.g., ibuprofen)** with **anticoagulants (e.g., warfarin)** can increase bleeding risk.
- Safer alternatives may exist (discuss with clinician/pharmacist).
- Monitoring and risk-benefit assessment are important.

## What to ask your clinician/pharmacist
- “Is there a safer alternative?”
- “What symptoms should trigger urgent care?”
- “Do I need monitoring (labs/vitals)?”
- “Is short-term use acceptable? What dose/duration is safest?”
